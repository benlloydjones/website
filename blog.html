20 August 2017

We had to solve a lot of problems using javascript as part of our homework this weekend. It was really fun going through each of them and wrestling with the steps I had to take to get from start to end of each function. My greatest frustration came from the .unshift() array method. I couldn't work out why I couldn't chain a .join() to it after I had inserted somthing at the beginning of my array. It just didn't make any sense. I resorted to spliting the steps out and putting them together one by one until they broke and then leaving it at the last step. Puzzling over what I was doing wrong. It turned out what I had done wrong was not read the documentation of the .unshift() method properly and realise that it didn't return the new array with it's new entry but instead returned its new length. Hopefully lesson learned: Read The Fucking Manual.
